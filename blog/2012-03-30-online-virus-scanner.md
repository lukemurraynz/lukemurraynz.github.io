If you suspect your computer is infected with malware but do not have antivirus software installed (or want a second opinion from a different vendor), **online virus scanners** can scan your system directly through your web browser.

These tools are provided by reputable security companies and are useful for:

- **Getting a second opinion** when your installed antivirus reports everything is clean but you still suspect an infection.
- **Scanning a computer that does not have antivirus installed.**
- **Checking specific files** against multiple antivirus engines.

## Recommended online scanners

- **[ESET Online Scanner](https://www.eset.com/int/home/online-scanner/)** - Full system scan using ESET's detection engine. Downloads a lightweight scanner agent.
- **[Kaspersky Threat Intelligence Portal](https://opentip.kaspersky.com/)** - Scan individual files and URLs for threats.
- **[Trend Micro HouseCall](https://www.trendmicro.com/en_us/forHome/products/housecall.html)** - Free online security scan for identifying and removing viruses, worms, spyware, and other threats.
- **[VirusTotal](https://www.virustotal.com/)** - Upload a file or enter a URL to scan it against 70+ antivirus engines simultaneously. The most useful tool for checking individual suspicious files.
- **[Microsoft Safety Scanner](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/defender-endpoint/safety-scanner-download)** - A downloadable tool from Microsoft that scans for and removes malware. Valid for 10 days after download.

## Tips

- **VirusTotal** is the best option for checking individual files, as it scans against dozens of engines at once.
- For a **full system scan**, ESET Online Scanner and Trend Micro HouseCall are the most thorough options.
- Online scanners are a supplement, not a replacement, for installed antivirus software. They are best used as a one-off check rather than ongoing protection.
- Some online scanners require a browser plugin or a small download to run. Make sure you are downloading from the vendor's official website.
