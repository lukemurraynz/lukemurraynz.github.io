eMachines and Gateway computers come with a **hidden recovery partition** on the hard drive that contains a factory image of the operating system. This allows you to restore the computer to its original state without needing a separate recovery disc.

## How to access the recovery partition

1. **Turn the computer on** (or restart it).
2. Immediately press **Alt + F10** at the same time, before the Windows logo appears.
3. The **recovery process** will start and guide you through the restoration.

> **Warning:** The recovery process will **erase all user data**, including documents, pictures, music, and installed applications. The computer will be restored to the same state it was in when you first turned it on.

## Before you begin

Make sure to back up any important files before starting the recovery. You can copy files to:

- An **external USB drive** or flash drive.
- A **cloud storage** service such as OneDrive or Google Drive.
- Another computer on your **local network**.

## If Alt + F10 does not work

- **Check BIOS settings:** Enter the BIOS (usually by pressing **F2** or **Del** at startup) and verify that the recovery partition feature, sometimes listed under a **D2D Recovery** option, is enabled.
- **Recovery partition may be damaged or removed:** If the partition was deleted or corrupted (for example, during a disk repartition), you will need to use a recovery disc instead. Contact Gateway/eMachines support or check if recovery media can be ordered.
